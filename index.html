Research

Since May 2016, I have been conducting research under the supervision of Dr. Dorothea Blostein in computer science at Queens University. My research is surrounding biomechanical simulation software for modelling tensegrity networks. Tensegrity is a modelling paradigm where a network of elements are held together by the counteraction of tension and compression. This is usually represented by rods and strings when being modelling physically. Tesengrity is particularly insightful for space exploration, architecture, and biomechanics when modelling fascial tissue.



My research has been a collaborative effort with engineers and biologists to develop improved extensions for the NASA Tensegrity Robotics Toolkit and PushMePullMe3D. This includes the use of machine learning to actuate structures, GUI improvements, and functional simulation improvements. Currently I am working on modelling cytoskeletal components and optimizing software simulation for these microscopic behaviours. Furthermore, I am also continuing to work on modelling of anatomical structures with the hope of improving understanding of human motion.
